A Motherly Relationship with Empathy: An Interview with my Mom♥

By: Abby Kalman and Jill Kalman


AK: To begin, if you had to define empathy, how would you describe it?


JK: Knowing the same feeling someone might be having because you experienced it yourself.

AK: Do you feel that moments of empathy and a shared experience among others are important? If so, do you feel we in society should talk about our shared experiences more in order to create a safe space?


JK: Very important. In fact critical to human relationships. Yes - talking about them gives an opportunity for people to connect and understand one another.

AK: As a mother, do you think having children has changed your perception of empathy? Do you think it has made you more deeply empathetic to not only your family, but other people around you?


JK: Yes. Having children is a common bond with other parents and being a mother provides an entirely different layer of perspective on the human condition. You can relate to other human beings as “a mother” and feel something common even in people you don’t know and may never meet. You can also relate to the feelings of motherhood with other mothers and be part of a community to support them because of your understanding of their emotions.

AK: What is a powerful moment where you felt empathetic towards someone, or you realized that someone was being empathetic towards you?


JK: I experienced great empathy with a friend who’s child attempted suicide. As a mother you can’t help but feel that on a very deep and powerful level.

AK: What values did you instill in your children that you believe made them empathetic people? Would you consider this answer advice for other parents? Do you think it’s important to instill values within your children that allow them to become empathetic?


JK: I taught you kids to care about others and be kind to others. And yes I think that leads to being empathetic and I think empathy is a key trait of a healthy personality.

AK: What do you think is the relationship between a mother and child in conjunction with empathy?


JK: I think a mother can relate to many things a child goes through because she was once a child herself and went through similar experiences. This provides the mother with a guide to assist the child but really allows her to be able to relate to that child. You need to have empathy to guide, develop and grow another human being.

AK: If any final thoughts, comments, or questions on anything discussed, whether that be empathy, human connection, shared experience, and your overall experiences and lessons you’ve had, feel free to add below.


JK: We probably currently live in a time where 1:1 in-person human connection is at its lowest point. Empathy is at risk due to this phenomenon. It’s critical to “walk in someone else’s shoes” and understand them for human connection.
